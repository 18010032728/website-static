Tracks and Awards

Your research project can participate in the following Tracks:

  1. Molecular Directed Evolution
  2. Pathway Directed Evolution
  3. Chassis Directed Evolution

Molecular Directed Evolution

The first class usually refers to directed evolution in a narrow sense, which sets specific molecules as a target.Here, molecules are not limited to enzymes. Any directed evolution project with molecules as an object meets the definition of this track.

Track I-I: Evolutionary Machines

Projects will develop new technologies related to molecular directed evolution, such as screening assays or variant generation.

There are no requirements for novel evolutionary output, but the iDEC requires the original design of molecular directed evolution technology or optimization of existing technology.

Track I-II: Evolutionary Outcomes

Projects will apply directed evolution technology to valuable target molecules. The goal of the project should be obtaining directed evolutionary output with scientific or industrial value.

There are no technological innovation requirements, but iDEC requires complete directed evolution circles and a superior directed evolution output.

Pathway Directed Evolution

The second class is the irrational design method often used to optimize metabolic pathways and synthetic circuits, which mainly screens the interactions of multiple genes. Simply put, this type of project involves the diversification and screening of the collaborative mode of a set of genes and biological macromolecules. This type of research may not change the primary structure or function of any biological macromolecules but screen the concentration ratio and combination of a group of biological macromolecules,such as the screening of multi-gene expression profiles.

Track II-I: Evolutionary Machines

Projects will aim at developing new technologies related to pathway directed evolution. There are no requirements for novel evolutionary output, but iDEC requires the original design of pathway directed evolution technology or optimization of existing technology.

Track II-II: Evolutionary Outcomes

Projects will apply directed evolution technology to valuable target pathways. The goal of the project should be obtaining directed evolutionary output with scientific or industrial value.

There are no technological innovation requirements, but iDEC requires complete directed evolution circles and a superior directed evolution output.

Chassis Directed Evolution

The last class is the directed evolution of the genome, which is closest to the natural evolution strategy. It is usually to accelerate the generation of mutations and exert selective pressure at the whole genome level.

Such projects do not need to pre-set a set of specific genes or biological macromolecules as targets. The teams can change the genetic background of chassis organisms by introducing random mutations into the whole genome, thereby achieving scientific or engineering goals. For example, the yeast genome SCRaMbLE.

Track III-I: Evolutionary Machines

For this track, the entries should be projects aiming at developing new technologies related to chassis directed evolution.

There are no requirements for novel evolutionary output, but iDEC requires the original design of directed evolution technology or existing technology optimization.

Track III-II: Evolutionary Outcomes

For this track, the entries should be projects that apply directed evolution technology to valuable target chassis. The goal of the project should be obtaining directed evolutionary output with scientific or industrial value.

There are no technological innovation requirements, but iDEC requires complete directed evolution circles and a superior directed evolution output.

Track Plus: Challenge

For this track, the Scientific Advisory Group and the Industry Advisory Group will propose a challenge. Participating teams can apply different platforms to conduct directed evolution research on designated scientific or industrial goals. The challenging advisory groups will evaluate the final results according to the corresponding standards set by them.

Note:

  • Our challenge will be released on Mar 1.
  • Each team can participate in multiple tracks.
  • The teams need to decide the ownership of Tracks before June 15.

1.General Awards

The general awards are mainly to encourage the participating teams to fully participate in all aspects of iDEC. Actively completing all aspects of iDEC will result in higher scores.

Gold Award (For Track I to III)

The team that ranks in the top 10% of the total score will receive a Gold award.

Silver Award (For Track I to III)

The team that ranks in the top 20%-10% of the total score will receive a Silver award.

Bronze Award (For Track I to III)

The team that ranks in the top 40%-20% of the total score will receive a Bronze award.

Community Award (For Track I to III)

For all the other qualified teams. The essential qualification condition is that the four display items are complete and conform to academic standards.

2.Track Awards

(1) Best New Evolution Machine
(3 teams for 3 tracks)

The judges nominate the candidates for this award and the judges will select the final winners after discussion and voting. The definition of this award is to reward innovative, proven, and feasible directed evolution technology.

The nomination criteria are:

  1. The technology proposed by the project must be an original technology or an optimized design based on existing technology.
  2. The technology must be proved feasible and superior to the current technology by experimental data in a real directed evolution case.
  3. If more than half of the judges believe that a project cannot meet the above standards, the project cannot be nominated.

The judges will discuss the innovation, feasibility and use value of this technology. They will vote on these three aspects separately. The team with the highest total number of votes wins.

(2) Best Evolution Outcome
(3 teams for 3 tracks)

The judges nominate the candidates for this award, and the judges will select the final winners after discussion and voting.

The definition of this award is to reward the best-directed evolution achievement. This is the grand prize of the directed evolution output track.

The nomination conditions are:

  1. The goal of directed evolution must reflect novelty, but the target molecule can be the product of previous directed evolution works.
  2. The result of evolution provides an unprecedented mutant, and its performance is better than any known version.
  3. The output has broad scientific significance or has high application value in industry. If more than half of the judges believe that a project cannot meet the above standards, the project cannot be nominated.

The judges will consider the above conditions together to vote on the nominated project, and the team with the most votes wins.
